The truth about website conversion rates

Most small business owners have no idea what a normal website conversion rate looks like. These are the real numbers — and the gap between where most sites sit and where they should be is where all the lost revenue lives.

0.5–1%
Where most underperforming sites sit
If your site has no clear CTA, slow load time, outdated design, or no trust signals — this is likely your reality. Most owners don't know because they've never measured it.
2–4%
Industry average conversion rate
A well-designed site with clear messaging, fast load time, visible contact info, and social proof should convert at 2–4% minimum. This is the baseline, not the goal.
5–8%
Top-performing local business sites
Sites with optimized landing pages, strong offers, review integration, live chat, and proper local SEO can hit 5–8%. At this level, your site is your best salesperson.
7x
The revenue multiplier of a great site
The difference between a 1% and 7% conversion rate — with the same traffic — is a 7x increase in leads. Not more ad spend. Not more traffic. Just a better site.
53%
Of visitors leave if your site loads slowly
More than half of mobile visitors abandon a page that takes over 3 seconds to load. That's traffic you're paying for or earning — and losing before they see anything.
75%
Judge credibility by website design alone
Three quarters of consumers form their opinion about a business's credibility based on website design — before reading a single word. Design is trust, and trust is revenue.
