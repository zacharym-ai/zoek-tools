The 3 rules of reputation management
⏱
Respond within 24 hours
Google factors response rate and speed into your local ranking. Every review deserves a response — yes, even old ones. Start with your most recent and work backward.
📞
Ask at peak happiness
The best time to ask for a review is right when the client is most satisfied — the moment the job is done, the result is visible, or the service exceeded their expectations. Not a week later.
📊
Track velocity, not just count
A steady flow of recent reviews beats a large number of old ones. Google weighs recency heavily. 5 new reviews this month matters more than 50 from 2 years ago.
Get more reviews
How to ask for reviews without being annoying
Most businesses never ask. The ones that do usually ask wrong. The ask needs to be personal, timely, and frictionless. Here's what actually works.
💬
Ask in person, then text the link
The most effective approach: ask verbally the moment they're happiest, then immediately text them your Google review link. The in-person ask gets buy-in. The text gives them frictionless follow-through.
📱
Make the link one tap away
Every extra click kills your conversion. Create a short URL (bit.ly or your own domain) that goes directly to your Google review form. Put it in your email signature, invoice footer, and post-service text.
🤝
Give them a reason to say yes
You don't need an incentive — you need a story. "Reviews are how small businesses like ours compete with the big guys" consistently outperforms generic requests. It makes the ask feel meaningful, not transactional.
Where to focus first
Platform priority guide
Not all review platforms are equal. Start where it moves the needle most for your business — usually Google — then expand from there once you have a system.
Google Business
Priority 1 — Every business
The most important review platform in existence. Google reviews directly impact your local search ranking, your Google Maps position, and whether someone calls you or your competitor.
Get your review link: Search your business on Google → click "Get more reviews" in your Business Profile dashboard. Send this link in every post-service message.
Facebook / Meta
Priority 2 — Consumer businesses
Facebook reviews (now called Recommendations) show up in local searches and are visible to your existing followers. High-value for restaurants, retail, and service businesses with a social presence.
Enable recommendations in your Business Page settings → Edit Page → Turn on Recommendations. Then share the link in your post-service follow-up.
Yelp
Priority 2 — Restaurants & local services
Critical for restaurants and home services in major metros. Yelp's algorithm is aggressive about filtering reviews — focus on making the ask feel organic and authentic, not scripted.
Warning: Yelp penalizes businesses that ask for reviews directly. Focus on making service excellent and letting reviews come naturally. Respond to every review you do get.
Houzz / Angi
Priority 3 — Home services
If you're in home services — remodeling, landscaping, plumbing, electrical — Houzz and Angi are active lead-gen platforms where reviews directly impact quote requests.
Build your profile fully, add project photos, and ask every satisfied client for a review on whichever platform they found you through.
Healthgrades / Zocdoc
Priority 3 — Healthcare
For medical and dental practices, patients search Healthgrades before calling. A strong profile with recent reviews builds trust before a prospect ever visits your website.
Claim and fully complete your Healthgrades profile. Add a post-appointment follow-up text or email asking patients to share their experience.
G2 / Capterra
Priority 3 — B2B / SaaS
For software and B2B services, G2 and Capterra are where buyers research before purchase decisions. Reviews here carry significant weight in enterprise sales cycles.
After a successful onboarding or milestone, email your customer with a direct link to your G2 profile and explain why reviews help your team grow.
The rules that don't change
Reputation management fundamentals
These principles apply regardless of platform, industry, or business size. The businesses that consistently win on reviews follow all of them.
⚡
Speed is a signal
Responding to reviews within 24 hours signals to both Google and future customers that you're engaged and care. Businesses that respond quickly are rated higher in local search results.
🧠
Never argue, never delete
Your response to a negative review is read by hundreds of future customers. They're watching to see if you're professional — not if you're right. Keep it short, calm, and move the conversation offline.
📅
Track your rating monthly
Set a monthly reminder to check your review count and average on every platform. Trends tell you more than snapshots. A declining velocity is a warning sign worth catching early.
🔗
Use keywords naturally in responses
When responding, include your service and location naturally: "Thanks for trusting Peak Plumbing with your emergency pipe repair in Irvine." It helps SEO without looking spammy. Natural, not robotic.
